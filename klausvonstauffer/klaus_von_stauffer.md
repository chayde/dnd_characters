## üìú Character Concept & Backstory

**"The Codex says you are standing in a statistically probable blast zone. You should move."**

### The Grunt Who Found a God
Klaus was never meant to be a wizard. He was a **heavy infantryman**‚Äîa rank-and-file soldier trained to hold the line, follow orders, and endure the mud and blood of siege warfare. He wasn't a hero; he was "reliable." He wore chainmail like a second skin and marched until his boots fell apart.

His life changed during the **Siege of Harrow's Peak**. His unit was decimated by arcane traps, and Klaus, the sole survivor, tumbled into a buried, sealed archive beneath the enemy tower. There, amidst the dust and silence, he found it: **The Tacticus Arcanum** (or simply, "The Codex").

### The Unlikely Apprentice
The Codex was a sentient grimoire belonging to a legendary, long-dead Archmage of War. It had waited centuries for a worthy apprentice‚Äîa prodigy of intellect and grace. Instead, it got Klaus.

Desperate to escape the ruins, Klaus opened the book, and the Codex, sensing raw potential (and desperation), unlocked a fraction of its power. It taught the grunt to cast a *Fire Bolt* not with a wand, but with the same muscle memory as firing a crossbow. It taught him that a *Shield* spell is just a better pavise.

### The Dynamics
*   **Klaus:** Approaches magic with soldierly pragmatism. He doesn't "weave the arcane"; he "fires for effect." He wears his old service armor because robes are for people who haven't been stabbed. He carries a sword because it doesn't run out of spell slots.
*   **The Codex:** A demanding, haughty, and brilliant instructor living in his head (and book). It constantly critiques his form and "crude" application of complex theorems.
    *   *Codex:* "You must visualize the ethereal vectors!"
    *   *Klaus:* "I'm just going to burn the door down."
    *   *Codex:* *Sighs psychically.* "Very well. But adjust for windage."

**Why He Adventures:** The Codex is guiding him. It claims Klaus needs "field data" to refine his technique, but Klaus suspects the book has a larger goal‚Äîperhaps its own resurrection or the defeat of an ancient rival. Klaus follows because he's tasted power, and he's never going back to digging latrines.

## ‚öîÔ∏è Combat Cheat Sheet (Level 9)

### The "Tank" Mode
*   **Passive AC:** 21 (Plate + Sentinel Shield + Defense Style)
*   **Reaction AC:** 26 (Cast *Shield*)
*   **Strategy:** Stand in the front line or hover above it. You are harder to hit than the Paladin. Use *Silvery Barbs* to protect allies or negate critical hits.

### The "Living Artillery" Mode
*   **Action:** *Fire Bolt* (2d10 Fire). Reflavored as "Magma Shot" or a heavy kinetic blast.
*   **Bonus Action:** *Manifest Mind* (Level 2 Feature).
    *   Creates a spectral version of The Codex within 60ft.
    *   **You can cast spells FROM its space.**
    *   *Tactics:* Send the spectral book into a room, stay behind full cover, and cast spells through it. You are an unseen mortar team.
*   **Telekinetic Shove:** Bonus Action to push/pull 5ft (DC 19 STR save). Use to peel enemies off allies or push them into hazards.

### The "Nuclear" Combo (CME + Scorching Ray)
1.  **Prep Turn:** Cast **Conjure Minor Elementals** (Action, Concentration). Creates a 15ft emanation.
2.  **Attack Turn:** Upcast **Scorching Ray** (Level 4 Slot = 5 rays).
    *   **Damage:** 5 Rays * (2d6 Fire + 2d8 Force/Cold/etc).
    *   **Total Potential:** **10d6 + 10d8**. ~90 Damage in one action.
    *   *Note:* You must be within 15ft of the target for CME damage to apply.

### The "Scribes" Gimmick (Awakened Spellbook)
*   **Feature:** Change Damage Type.
*   **Effect:** When you cast a wizard spell with a spell slot, you can temporarily replace its damage type with a type that appears in another spell in your book.
*   **Dictionary:**
    *   *Dragon's Breath* (Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Poison)
    *   *Erupting Earth* (Bludgeoning)
    *   *Thunderwave* (Thunder)
    *   *Magic Missile* (Force)
    *   *Silvery Barbs* (Psychic)
